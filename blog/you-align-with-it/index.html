Resume Thoughts

"The disagreement between the two camps is loud because they disagree about how the designing should go, but underneath the loudness sits a much larger agreement, which is that the participants in the debate are the ones doing the designing and everyone else is what gets designed for."

So Daniel Tan cuts through the alignment debate (source). The fight between safety teams and accelerationists is a family quarrel. Both sides assume they're in charge of the future, and the rest of us are just along for the ride.

I clipped that in May. A few weeks later I clipped another link: U of T researchers demonstrating an AI worm that could target any online device. On that thread, someone pointed to what they'd already built: a 4MB bug-hunting harness called Rook (source), designed for pentesting, trivially adaptable into a self-propagating worm that burns through compromised API keys. They've already made a self-modifiable version.

These two clips belong together.

The alignment debate argues about how to constrain AI. The worm demo shows what happens when the constraints don't hold -- or when they're never applied in the first place. The debate assumes someone is in control, designing the guardrails. The worm works because it doesn't ask permission.

"I read the other day that the elites are beginning to talk in terms of a permanent underclass."

It connects the other two. The alignment debate and the worm are both conversations about who holds power. The safety teams and the builders are the elites of the AI world. The rest of us are users, subjects, targets. The fierceness of the argument disguises the fact that the argument is not with us at all.

The worm is what happens when the elites stop arguing and start building. It's not malicious in intent but it doesn't need to be. It just needs to be small, adaptable, and indifferent. The underclass doesn't get aligned; it gets aligned with.

The real question is not how to align AI. It's whether you're in the room designing the future or being designed for.
